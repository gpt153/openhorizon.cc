## üí° Tips & Best Practices

### Writing Effective Seed Prompts

‚úÖ **Good prompts include:**
- Theme or topic (e.g., "digital skills", "climate action")
- Target group (e.g., "youth ages 16-20", "youth workers")
- Countries or locations
- Rough duration

**Examples:**
```
"Youth exchange about climate action in Sweden, Poland, and Germany for ages 16-20"
"Training course for youth workers on digital storytelling, 5 days in Barcelona"
"Mobility project about social entrepreneurship for young adults from rural areas"
```

‚ùå **Avoid:**
- Single words ("project", "youth")
- Overly vague ("something about environment")
- Too specific/detailed (leave room for AI creativity)

---

### Using Elaboration Effectively

**Ask specific questions:**
- "What activities would work for 15-year-olds instead of 18-year-olds?"
- "Focus more on hands-on workshops and less on lectures"
- "Add an outdoor team-building component"

**Build conversationally:**
```
You: "What activities suit 15-year-olds?"
AI: [Suggests age-appropriate activities]

You: "Focus more on workshops, less on lectures"
AI: [Refines activity list]

You: "Add outdoor components"
AI: [Adds outdoor activities]
```

---

### Structuring Programmes

**Best practices:**
1. **Balance activity types** - Mix workshops, discussions, outdoor, cultural
2. **Include breaks** - 15-30 min between major activities
3. **Daily reflections** - End each day with learning reflection
4. **Build flexibility** - Buffer time for when things run late
5. **Consider energy** - Complex activities in morning, social in afternoon

**Sample day structure:**
```
09:00-09:30   Energizer
09:30-11:00   Workshop 1
11:00-11:30   Coffee break
11:30-13:00   Workshop 2
13:00-14:30   Lunch
15:00-17:00   Group activity
17:00-17:30   Daily reflection
18:30-19:30   Dinner
20:00-22:00   Free time / cultural evening
```

---

### Budget Calculator Tips

- **Verify distances** - Use Google Maps to confirm km between cities
- **Check unit costs** - Ensure you're using current year's Erasmus+ rates
- **Account for special needs** - Budget for inclusion support
- **Test scenarios** - Try different participant numbers or destinations
- **Green travel bonus** - Trains/buses can unlock extra funding days

---

### Vendor Search Tips

**Accommodation:**
- Start searching 3-6 months in advance
- Prioritize: location ‚Üí facilities ‚Üí price ‚Üí flexibility
- Ask about group discounts and Erasmus+ support

**Travel:**
- Consider green travel first (trains/buses get EU bonus)
- Group bookings often get discounts
- Book flexible tickets if visas may delay travel

**Food:**
- Always ask about dietary accommodations
- Request sample menus
- Verify group capacity
- Check delivery/service flexibility

## üéØ Typical Workflow

Here's how most users create projects:

### Week 1: Ideation
1. **Generate seeds** - Brainstorm project ideas with AI
2. **Save to garden** - Store promising concepts
3. **Elaborate** - Refine your favorite ideas
4. **Convert to project** - Create full project structure

### Week 2-3: Planning
5. **Build programme** - Create detailed daily schedules
6. **Calculate budget** - Get accurate cost estimates
7. **Search vendors** - Find accommodation, travel, food options
8. **Request quotes** - Contact vendors for pricing

### Week 4: Finalization
9. **Review and refine** - Ensure all sections complete
10. **Partner review** - Share with partners for input
11. **Export** - Generate application documents
12. **Submit** - Upload to National Agency portal

**Total time: 4-6 hours** (vs 40-60 hours manually!) ‚ö°

## ‚ùì FAQ

**Q: Is OpenHorizon free?**
A: Check current pricing on our website. We offer free tiers and plans for organizations.

**Q: Do I need technical knowledge?**
A: No! OpenHorizon is designed for project coordinators, not developers.

**Q: Can I work on multiple projects?**
A: Yes! Create and manage unlimited projects simultaneously.

**Q: Is my data safe?**
A: Yes. GDPR compliant, encrypted, stored on EU servers.

**Q: Can I collaborate with team members?**
A: Organization-level collaboration is planned. Currently, share via export.

**Q: What Erasmus+ actions are supported?**
A: KA1 (Learning Mobility) and KA2 (Cooperation Partnerships). KA3 coming soon.

**Q: Does OpenHorizon guarantee approval?**
A: No. We help you plan and document, but approval depends on National Agency evaluation.

**Q: Can I export to Word/PDF?**
A: Yes! Export to PDF, DOCX, and XLSX formats.

**Q: Is there a mobile app?**
A: Not yet. Web app works on mobile browsers but optimized for desktop/tablet.
